Jacob Weston “Jake” Kincaid

Role: Anchor, protector, and one of the Five Oaks of Five Oaks.

Basic Information

  • Known As: Jake
  • Birthday: November 3, 1867
  • Wife: Krysten Irene “Krys” Callahan Kincaid
  • Children: Kai Weston Kincaid and Wesley Jon Kincaid
  • Grandchild: Wesley Lucas Kincaid
  • Home: Five Oaks Ranch

Family

Jake comes from a childhood where blood did not mean safety. His father was absent. His mother remarried, and the house she brought him into became a place ruled by cruelty. At thirteen, Jake left with nothing but the clothes he wore. The family he later builds at Five Oaks is the opposite of what he came from.

Physical Description

Jake has dark hair, dark eyes, and the kind of face people remember even when he says very little. By his early fifties, gray threads through his hair and beard, but he still carries the same quiet authority that has defined him since youth.

Personality

Jake is quiet, loyal, controlled, protective, and deeply private. He does not trust easily, but once his loyalty is earned, it is absolute. His deepest fear is becoming the kind of man who makes a child or woman shrink inside his presence.

The Five Oaks of Five Oaks

Krys: Krys is Jake’s wife, partner, and the person who changes what he believes love can survive.

Matt: Matt and Jake understand each other in the old, hard way of men who have seen danger together and know the other will not run.

Luke: Luke brings air into Jake’s world. Without Luke, Five Oaks would have less warmth and fewer ways back from the edge.

Jace: Jace is pressure in human form, testing the integrity of the family they are building.

Role in the Saga

Jake is one of the five foundations of the saga. He is the man who ends danger when danger leaves no other road, but he is defined just as much by restraint, loyalty, and the work of choosing something better than what shaped him.

Legacy

Jacob Weston Kincaid’s legacy is not violence. It is restraint. It is the choice to stand between harm and family without becoming the kind of man who creates harm in the first place.
